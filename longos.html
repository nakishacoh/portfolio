Online Grocery Shopping - UX Case Study

Role

Research, Ideation,Visuals, Prototyping

Timeline

Nov 2018

Team

Shawn Li, Cleo Lant, Sanya Sabu

Tools Used

Illustrator, Photoshop, XD

I had the opportunity to work closely with three classmates on the usability of Longo's grocery website. The purpose of the analysis was to understand and apply interaction design concepts to the structure of their user flow. Due to my energetic personality, I was the test subject used to capture the pain points and ease of interaction. 

Task

As the lead designer of the team, I was asked to create a new user flow, empathy map, job story, and a low fidelity version of the improvements. 

Research

1. Job Story

Prior to conducting our user research, we created job stories to understand the needs and wants of a user wanting to shop for groceries online. Our job stories suggest that there are a diverse personas with common patterns of prioritizing individual needs and time. 

2. Emapthy Map

To go further in depth of the user's experience, my team conducted a user profile (moi) to understand my perspective before beginning my shopping experience. 

3. User Interaction

My group recorded several videos and wrote the ease of interaction and pain points evaluating my verbal and facial reaction using the application. 

    Ease of Interaction

  • Shows accepted methods of payment in the website footer
  • Updates number of items and cost of cart in top right hand corner of the page
  • Too much information in the hamburger menu, but the categories are good
  • Easy to find healthy and prepared foods, harder to find snacks
  • Quick view of items

    Pain Points

  • No direct link to start shopping for delivery in the top navigation on homepage - a picture with a link when you scroll down the main page to another website, confusing why it has a different brand
  • Inconsistent with what is a pop up and what you can hover over
  • Order minimum $50 for delivery - when you click continue shopping it take you to the last category you added an item from
  • The first item you add doesn’t give confirmation it was added because you have to click the business or home button too, a little confusing that there is no confirmation button

4. Competitor Analysis

To understand the market of online grocery shopping we researched competitors within the Ontario region. Luckily for Longo's, the market of competitors is small. The only store that provides a similar service is Loblaws.

    Similarities

  • Same price for pickup
  • Nice colours and images
  • Updated visual when items are added to the cart
  • So much space taken up by the header and menu that the actual items/ page is pretty slim
  • Adding items to cart with live updates
  • Increasing and decreasing item quantity with plus and minus tabs
  • Navigation is pretty simple
  • Ability to set up recurring order

    Differences

  • Links in navigation have sub navigations, which hovers and shows specific group type
  • Nutritional information
  • Pop up blurs out background and everything stays in place
  • When product is added a sticker is over the product that says ‘in cart’
  • Checkout button changes colour when cart minimum is met
  • Dropdown list for cart
  • Chosen pickup time and place is shown next to cart icon in nav

Reflection

Creating the design elements under a limited time frame was a challenge that tested my quick thinking skills and ability to deliver. I have learned to have patience with the process of revisions and how easy it is to miss simple details in design that reveal major pain points for the user.
