In my Interaction Design class, we were assigned to create an empathy map for a popular product. The main objective of this assignment was to understand human behaviour, goals, and the how the product fulfills the user's needs. I had the pleasure of interviewing my pal, Collin Busschots, all the way from Belgium.

Research

1. User Profile

I had a lovely conversation with Collin discussing his experience using the application. I tested his cultural behaviourl patterns to evaluate Spotify's success in user experience. The questions I asked Collin were all the main topics in an empathy map: feelings, task, influence, pain points, and goals. After collecting the first round of information, I asked Collin from the list of points mentioned what he thought were main motivations for each topic. I enjoyed conducting the interview, because it was an interesting way to test my ability to make the interaction between the researcher and interviewee open and friendly. Here are some sample questions.

  1. 1. How do you feel when you open the application? (i.e. calm, familiar, easy to navigate)​
  2. 2. Where and when do you find yourself using the app the most?​
  3. 3. Any difficulties using the application?
  4. 4. Who or what influced you the most to purchase an account?
  5. 5. What part of the application do you enjoy the most? (i.e. Facebook friends list, playlist creation)

Iterations

1. Sketch

I then created a sketch of my mockup. My thought process first led me to draw out Collin's profile on my Android

Problem

  • The font size is too small for a user to see
  • Alignment is off within the header of the profile
  • The gradient of the topics do not follow the theme of Spotify
  • Limited whitespace
  • Shape of user photo is not circular
  • Inconsistent theme of fonts

Solution

  • Creating multiple mockups to write out the points of each topic
  • Alignment items properly
  • Switch the background colour to the Spotify theme
  • Make the user photo circular
  • Stick to two theme of fonts

Design

With great critique and constant revision, a final version was complete with additional mockups

Relfection

After revisiting my designs and getting valuable feedback, I gave further consideration to cleaning up my errors and observing the development of my improvements. The main problem I faced was being able to give proportionate space aligning the elements. Being one of my earlier projects, I had little knowledge of the design program and interaction methods. Creating this empathy map is revolutionary to my design skills and UI rules.

As for user research, I was enlightened with the success of Spotify's understanding of human behaviour. Their product had little user frustrations, because they carried out the goals, productivity, and environment of their user's needs.
