As a lover of music, I realized the problem of artists not being able to fulfill their career as an independent artist due to low streaming royalties. When the opportunity came to design this experience, creating a user friendly product for artists to continue their passion seemed natural.

Problem

With the complexity of laws and regulation in the music industry, many artists find it difficult to make a living with major financial cuts (or none at all) streaming their music. For instance, Spotify pays between $0.006 to $0.0084 USD per stream to the owner of the music, which is then cut between the record label, managers, owners, artists, and other contributors. The average payout for an artist with 1,000,000 plays ranges $4,000-$5,000 USD.

How can small artists continue to make music with little payout?

Challenge

From my experience volunteering and attending numerous of small concerts, what listeners appreciate the most about shows are the moments you experience. Hearing your favourite artists or singing with the crowd brings warmth and astoundment to my senses.

Which brought on the concept of a streaming service that allows listeners to hear recordings from live events.

How might this system relate to the user demographic: edgy, underground, and modern?

Research

Starting from my circle friends who are in the music scene, my friends, Michael Bara and Alyssa Hankey allowed me to record them for knowledge of their likes and dislikes with music streaming services and events. Interviewing users was used to find problems using competitor applications and design aesthetics to create elements efficient for demographics mainly using the application.

Objective

  1. 1. Understand their taste in music
  2. 2. Learn environment upbringing/cultural background to know the demographics of the application
  3. 3. Figure out frustrations with streaming service aesthetic and user flow
  4. 4. Recognize motivations to use music streaming apps​
  5. 5. Hobby vs. career​

1. Interview Questions

  1. 1. What's your experience in music?​
  2. 2. Do you like to watch recordings of shows you attended or shows of artists performance?​
  3. 3. What type of performance space do you enjoy the most? (venues, arenas, or house basements)
  4. 4. What streaming services do you like to listen to music?
  5. 5. What genre of music do you like to listen to?
  1. 1. As a solo artists, do you support yourself outside of your passion?​
  2. 2. Do you post your videos/live audio online from live shows?
  3. 3. What services do you post your music online?
  4. 4. What genre of music do you perform?
  5. 5. Describe the type of people that attend your shows?

2. Mapping User Data

After conducting the interviews, I wanted to gather the demographic and cultural similarities to understand the identity that would be portrayed through design. The identity of the brand would intertwine a connection between the music industry and underground culture. First, I created thought of names that had the feeling of its own personality.

Second, I gathered the interview questions and cultural groups that influence the aesthetic of the application. Putting together the groups will narrow down the demographics to finely know who, what, and why people will use the application.

Audience

People who detach themselves from mainstream cultural customs. They attend shows in small venues to hear local artists. These shows are diverse culturally as audience members as have extreme to neutral left wing political and social standards.

Genre

The main genres of music are alternative, hip-hop, rock, folk, and poetry. What is strongly disliked with the audience members is country music.

Aesthetic

The themes that represents originality, creativity, and secrecy is the impression that best fits the audience. Colours that fit this area are known as subjective - colours that present one's personal preference and taste.

3. Results

People who are mainly in attendance at small shows are those who involve themselves in the alternative/independent community. This includes punks, rappers, alternative, and anyone who does not involve in mainstream media. Their appreciation for art, vintage looks, and rebellion against leftist politics surrounds the cultural aesthetic. As for artists, their need for artistic expression and having full control of their music is a main concern. The needs of a casual listener is against the use of ads and propaganda of huge artists.

Design Elements

  1. 1. Include images of artists performing in small/intimate performances
  2. 2. Include colours that are subjective
  3. 3. Primary: Yellow
  4. 4. Secondary: Purple and pink
  5. 5. Layouts similar to most music streaming apps in regards to user comfort level of interaction

Iterations

1. User Flow

I took the opportunity to use class assignments to help progress and brainstorm.

2. Low-Fidelity Wireframe

I decided to change my application into a mobile versions after I reviewed notes from user research. Many users mentioned their use of music services on mobile more than actual desktops due to their lifestyle on the road.

3. High Fidelity

Problem

  • 1. Having a repetition of images in the Playlist/Album page is not necessary. In a critique session, this was a major dislike among classmates
  • 2. Align icons better
  • 3. Back button needed in Playlist/Album page
  • 4. Increase font size under images

Design

Reflection

This assignment has more value to me than a class project, it involves two things that I have a passion for: music and helping people pursue their creative endeavours. The ability to connect people and do something for the greater good from an empathetic viewpoint makes the value of user experience a priority for the design features. Going through the process of interviewing people in the music field has taught me a lot about the need to really know my audience and the benefits of the appearance. I hope the development of this project continues and evolves into something tangible as design and user perspective changes.
